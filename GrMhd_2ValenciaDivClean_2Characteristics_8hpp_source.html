19 /*!
20  * \brief Compute the characteristic speeds for the Valencia formulation of
21  * GRMHD with divergence cleaning.
22  *
23  * Obtaining the exact form of the characteristic speeds involves the solution
24  * of a nontrivial quartic equation for the fast and slow modes. Here we make
25  * use of a common approximation in the literature (e.g. \ref char_ref "[1]")
26  * where the resulting characteristic speeds are analogous to those of the
27  * Valencia formulation of the 3-D relativistic Euler system
28  * (see RelativisticEuler::Valencia::characteristic_speeds),
29  *
30  * \f{align*}
31  * \lambda_2 &= \alpha \Lambda^- - \beta_n,\\
32  * \lambda_{3, 4, 5, 6, 7} &= \alpha v_n - \beta_n,\\
33  * \lambda_{8} &= \alpha \Lambda^+ - \beta_n,
34  * \f}
35  *
36  * with the substitution
37  *
38  * \f{align*}
39  * c_s^2 \longrightarrow c_s^2 + v_A^2(1 - c_s^2)
40  * \f}
41  *
42  * in the definition of \f$\Lambda^\pm\f$. Here \f$v_A\f$ is the Alfvén
43  * speed. In addition, two more speeds corresponding to the divergence cleaning
44  * mode and the longitudinal magnetic field are added,
45  *
46  * \f{align*}
47  * \lambda_1 = -\alpha - \beta_n,\\
48  * \lambda_9 = \alpha - \beta_n.
49  * \f}
50  *
51  * \note The ordering assumed here is such that, in the Newtonian limit,
52  * the exact expressions for \f$\lambda_{2, 8}\f$, \f$\lambda_{3, 7}\f$,
53  * and \f$\lambda_{4, 6}\f$ should reduce to the
54  * corresponding fast modes, Alfvén modes, and slow modes, respectively.
55  * See \ref mhd_ref "[2]" for a detailed description of the hyperbolic
56  * characterization of Newtonian MHD.
57  *
58  * \anchor char_ref [1] C.F Gammie, J.C McKinney, G. Tóth, HARM: A Numerical
59  * Scheme for General Relativistic Magnetohydrodynamics, ApJ.
60  * [589 (2003) 444](http://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1086/374594/meta)
61  *
62  * \anchor mhd_ref [2] A. Dedner et al., Hyperbolic Divergence Cleaning for the
63  * MHD Equations, J. Comput. Phys.
64  * [175 (2002) 645](https://doi.org/10.1006/jcph.2001.6961)
65  */
